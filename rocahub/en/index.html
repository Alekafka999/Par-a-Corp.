Digital infrastructure for small producers to sell better, operate better, and make clearer decisions.

Roca Hub is designed to connect small producers, local businesses, and practical market intelligence through a simple experience. The thesis is straightforward: less operational friction, more commercial visibility, and a digital layer that respects the reality of the field.

Real pain

Potential

Opportunity

Local agriculture still runs with too much friction for small-scale producers.

At the edge of the market, the problem is rarely just technology. The challenge is combining organization, commercial intelligence, and access to opportunities without forcing a level of complexity that producers do not have time to absorb.

Roca Hub is designed for environments where margins are tight, time is scarce, and decision-making must be simple enough to actually happen.

Low commercial predictability

Pricing, demand, and sales channels are often decided with limited reference points. That drives waste, compresses margins, and limits sustainable growth.

Fragmented tools

Management, communication, relationships, and logistics are frequently spread across notebooks, spreadsheets, chat threads, and improvised routines.

Weak territorial integration

Producers, local commerce, distribution, and regional opportunities rarely operate through a shared flow. The result is underused market potential.

Product

Roca Hub organizes what is currently scattered and turns routine into business intelligence.

The goal is not to add complexity to the field. It is to deliver a lean digital layer that helps producers see better, negotiate better, and operate with greater consistency.

The product can start simple for fast adoption and gain depth as new partners, data, and workflows join the network.

Investment Thesis

Why this project may be compelling for smart capital.

Roca Hub combines a concrete problem, progressive adoption potential, and a build strategy that fits Parça Corp's lean execution model.

This is not a glamour thesis. It is a thesis around badly solved friction in a large, fragmented sector that is still underserved by simple digital experiences.

Large market, recurring pain

Small producers face a repeating set of issues across different regions: organization, sales, predictability, and access to market.

Low-friction build

Because it is being developed inside a venture builder with intensive AI support, the project gains speed in prototyping, research, content, and iteration with a lean structure.

Network as an accumulating advantage

Each new local node increases the utility of the product. Intelligence improves with usage, and value grows with ecosystem density.
